Dates and Times
toc
Requirements
Products should display dates and times formatted according to the regional conventions of the locale of the current operating system (for desktop or touch applications) or browser (for web applications).
Dates and times can be represented in short, medium, long or full formats. It's up to the product owner to decide which format is more appropriate for each area of the product.
What's affected by locale changes
  • Positioning of day, month and year
  • Use of 12-hr vs 24-hr formats
  • Use of suffixes to indicate time of day (e.g. A.M./P.M)
  • Capitalization of month names

How to test
  1. Identify all areas of the product that display dates and times.
  2. Change the environment locale, in order to verify that the date and time formats change and match accordingly in such areas.
    • Instructions on how to change the environment locale can be found here.
    • A comprehensive list of locale-specific dates and time formats can be found in the CLDR site.
  3. For example, to check what the date and time formats for the French (Canada) locale is, in the CLDR page (link provided in the previous step), type 'French' in the 'Locale' search box, then select 'Canada' from the list of countries. Then, select 'Date and Time' > 'Gregorian', and scroll down to the 'Formats - Standard' sections.
